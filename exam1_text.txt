A.  Moving charge generates a magnetic field, and a moving magnetic field causes charges to move.
B. Atomic nuclei have a quantum mechanical property of "spin" that can be thought of as a small magnetic 
field around the nucleus created as if the positive charge of the nucleus were circulating.
C. We care about the nuclei 1H and 13C since these are commonly found in organic molecules and they 
have spin quantum numbers of 3/2.
D. Electromagnetic radiation of enough or more than enough energy can be absorbed by a nucleus and 
excite it from the lower energy +1/2 spin state to the higher energy -1/2 spin state, a process referred to 
as resonance.
E. Electromagnetic radiation of only the exactly right amount of energy can be absorbed by a nucleus and 
excite it from the lower energy +1/2 spin state to the higher energy -1/2 spin state, a process referred to as 
resonance.
F. The distance between peaks in a split signal is called chemical shift.
I. For alkyl groups with freely rotating C-C bonds, splitting by n adjacent H atoms will give n+1 peaks.
M. The H atoms of relatively acidic functional groups (alcohols, carboxylic acids, amines) exchange 
rapidly, so they often do not split the signals of adjacent hydrogens.
G. Different hydrogen atoms in a molecule take different amounts of energy to flip their spins, and the 
different energies can be correlated to structure of the molecule.
H. The distance between a signal and the standard TMS is called coupling constant.
J. For H atoms on the C atoms of three-membered rings or on the sp2 hybridized C atoms of alkenes, 
splitting by n adjacent H atoms will always give (n x n)+1 peaks.
K. The signal for a CH2  group next to a chiral center will never be split, it will be a singlet.
L. All geminal H atoms split each other, giving rise to double peaks with large chemical shifts.
N. NMR stands for No More Regrets, time to develop a plan for lifelong physical fitness.
